I come from **Gunjur**, a town on the coast of **The Gambia**. When I try to explain where I’m from, I usually start with how it *feels*: close-knit, familiar, and grounded.

## What shaped me

Growing up in Gunjur taught me a few things that still show up in my work:

- **Community matters.** People look out for each other, and you learn early that progress is rarely a solo project.
- **Resourcefulness is a skill.** You figure out how to make things work with what you have—then you improve it over time.
- **Show up and contribute.** Whether it’s family, neighbors, or local initiatives, participation is part of daily life.

## A long view

Leaving home for school and work gave me new tools, but Gunjur gave me the *why*. It’s where I learned to take responsibility, stay curious, and keep a long view—especially when problems are hard and the path isn’t obvious.

## Staying connected

No matter where I live now, I carry Gunjur with me—in the way I think about impact, in the patience to iterate, and in the desire to build things that are useful for real people.
